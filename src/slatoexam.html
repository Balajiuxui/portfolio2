Designing Exams That Make Students Smile

This case study focuses on the design of Slato Exams, a mobile-first digital assessment platform built for rural students across India. The goal was to provide a simple, gamified, and accessible experience for students taking exams, while equipping internal teams with a powerful yet easy-to-use admin portal. The result is a scalable product that empowers young learners and supports digital learning in underserved communities.

Role

Senior Product Designer

Year

Platforms

Mobile App (for students) | Web Portal (for internal admins)

EdTech / Rural Education

Why This Project Mattered

In rural India, students often lack access to structured digital assessments. Existing platforms are typically built for urban, tech-savvy users — not for children with limited device access or digital literacy.

Our goals:

Build a mobile-first exam app that students can use independently

Make exams engaging and motivating with a reward system

Allow the Slato team to manage users, content, and reports with ease

My Role in This Journey

As the sole designer, I was responsible for:

Researching with 15+ rural teachers

Creating both the student exam experience and internal admin tools

Designing UX flows, wireframes, and visual components

Collaborating with dev, QA, education, and sales teams

Iterating quickly based on real classroom feedback

Understanding the Users

What We Did:

Conducted 1:1 interviews with teachers and volunteers

Observed children using shared devices (phones/tablets)

Tested early flows with first-time users

Key Insights:

UIs needed to be minimal and icon-driven

Teachers needed a no-training-required backend

Feedback (visual + verbal)was essential for student confidence

Designing a Simple MVP

To move fast, we built an MVP with just the essentials:

Student login

Exam list and start button

Clean, mobile-friendly exam screens

Certificates and detailed performance reports after submission

Design Priorities:

Reduce friction at every step

Keep screens light and focused

Ensure scalability across regions and learning centers

Gamifying the Exam Experience

To make exams less intimidating and more rewarding:

I designed a coin-based reward system

Students earned coins after completing each test

Positive reinforcement encouraged repeat use

This simple gamification strategy helped boost motivation and test completion rates.

Building the Admin Portal

The internal team needed tools to:

Upload exams

Track submissions

Manage users for education and sales

Admin Design Features:

Role-based access (sales, education, ops)

Intuitive layouts with clear actions

Simple filters and report views

Easy scaling for future use

Iterating With Feedback

We continuously refined the app based on:

Teacher insights by coordinators

Field testing by coordinators

Internal team usage feedback

Resulting Improvements:

Simplified buttons and instructions

Added visual cues for progress

Created better reporting visibility for students and teachers

The Impact We Made

Student Access - 85% of students completed exams without adult supervision after first session

Engagement Boost - Test completion rates improved by 40% after adding the coin reward system

Report Clarity - 100% of students received auto-generated certificates and score reports

Team Workflow - Admins reduced exam setup time by 60% using the web portal (no dev support)

“Based on internal team feedback and field observations.”

What I Learned (and Loved)

Designing for first-time tech users requires empathy, not assumptions

Gamificationadds meaningful motivation — even in simple forms

Admin tools are often invisible, but crucial for scale and sustainability

A good design process includes feedback from every layer — student, teacher, ops
