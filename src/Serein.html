Empowering Inclusive Learning with Serein LMS

This case study highlights the design of Serein LMS, a learning management system built for corporate training on workplace safety, gender rights, and DEI (Diversity, Equity, Inclusion). Developed by Krishworks for enterprise clients, Serein helps companies educate employees about workplace harassment, compliance, and inclusive culture.

As the lead designer, I was responsible for the entire product design — from the design system to the LMS web application and marketing website. The platform is now used by large companies including TATA, Siemens, Flipkart, and Spotify.

Why This Project Mattered

Serein’s core mission is to educate corporate employees about their rights and responsibilities in the workplace. It tackles real issues such as:

Gender equality & anti-harassment compliance

Inclusive hiring and representation metrics

Culturally sensitive training with certificates

Legal education around DEI policies

It’s not just another LMS — it’s a platform designed for real cultural change in organizations.

My Role in This Journey

I worked as the sole UX UI designer and led every stage of the UX/UI process:

Built a design system for consistent components and scalability

Created the marketing website to explain the platform’s value

Collaborated with stakeholders, developers, and QA teams

Designed the entire LMS web app — student side and admin dashboard

Ensured responsive, accessible layouts for real-world use

Understanding the Problem

Before jumping into visuals, I worked with the client team to understand:

What makes DEI training effective (and engaging)?

How do corporate teams monitor DEI progress?

What do employees need to trust and use the LMS?

I mapped these into four design priorities:

Clarity: No jargon-heavy UI

Inclusivity: Soft visuals and gender-neutral themes

Scalability: A system that works for teams of 10 or 10,000

Compliance: Every action leads to reportable, measurable outcomes

Designing the Design System

To make the platform scalable across modules, I created a robust design system in Figma:

Color tokens for accessibility

Type hierarchy for courses and metrics

Reusable components: cards, tabs, dashboards, icons

Mobile responsiveness baked in

Navigation standards for all user types (learner, admin, manager)

LMS Product Highlights

Culture Index

Visual maturity scale showing where the organization stands in gender inclusion — from Initiate to Accelerator.

Clear, gamified feedback with color-coded tips and next steps

Encourages action and progression without judgment

Diversity Metrics

Helps HR teams visualize representation across gender and teams.

Custom filters by year, department, or role

Designed for executives and DEI managers to view impact quickly

Interactive Learning Modules

Course screens were designed like a modern dashboard — not like old-school LMS.

Content in mixed formats: videos, cards, quizzes, and games

Real-time course status and gamified progress bars

Accessibility considerations: color contrast, keyboard nav, large tap areas
